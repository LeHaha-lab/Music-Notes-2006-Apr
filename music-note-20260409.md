## 4. Recommended Piano Listening Path

### Entry Route by Repertory

The recovered discussion suggested a practical order for new listeners:

1. Start with Chopin, Beethoven, and Mozart for melodic clarity and immediate accessibility.
2. Move to Liszt and Rachmaninoff to hear piano virtuosity and large-scale Romantic sound.
3. Continue with Debussy, Ravel, and Bach to develop sensitivity to color, texture, and structure.
4. Finish with Prokofiev to enter 20th-century rhythmic and harmonic language.

### Entry Route by Pianist

| Stage | Pianists | Suggested Listening Focus |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First entry | Artur Rubinstein, Zimerman | Chopin nocturnes, ballades, concertos |
| Strong personality stage | Horowitz, Argerich | Rachmaninoff Piano Concerto No. 3, Prokofiev Piano Concerto No. 3 |
| Structural listening stage | Richter, Gilels, Brendel | Beethoven sonatas, late Schubert sonatas |
| Tone-color stage | Michelangeli | Debussy preludes, Ravel |
| Individual interpretation stage | Gould | Bach *Goldberg Variations* |
